Our History & Legacy

The history of Negros Oriental State University (NORSU) in Bayawan dates back to its establishment as the Tolong Junior High School (TJHS) in 1947. The school was initially located in the heart of the Municipality of Bayawan, near Rizal Park and the Amphi-theater. It was later renamed TJHS and transferred to the Balabag site, where it became the Bayawan National High School and City Agriculturist Office. In 1950, it was converted into Negros Oriental Rural School (NORS) and later into Negros Oriental National Agricultural School (NONAS) in 1966. The school was eventually integrated into the Central Visayas Polytechnic College (CVPC) in 2000, which was later converted into NORSU in 2005.
