Accessible for everyone

Accessibility Statement

Our commitment to an inclusive digital experience for all visitors, including people with disabilities.

Inclusive Design

We build with WCAG 2.1 principles in mind: perceivable, operable, understandable, robust.

Keyboard Friendly

Navigation and controls are operable via keyboard with visible focus states.

Assistive Tech Support

Semantic HTML, labels, and alt text to work well with screen readers and other tools.

What we focus on

  • Clear hierarchy with headings, lists, and landmarks.
  • High-contrast text, scalable font sizes, and responsive layouts.
  • Alt text for meaningful images; decorative images are hidden from assistive tech.
  • Caption-ready media and descriptive link text where feasible.

Keyboard tips

  • Tab / Shift + Tab to move focus between links and controls.
  • Enter or Space to activate buttons and links.
  • Esc to close menus or dialogs where applicable.

Standards

We aim to meet or exceed WCAG 2.1 AA where practical and continue improving based on feedback and audits.

Report an issue

If you experience barriers or have suggestions, please tell us so we can address them promptly.
